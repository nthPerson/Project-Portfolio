About

I'm an applied AI engineer and researcher who builds systems where software meets the physical world — agentic AI pipelines, machine-learning models, and the robotics and edge hardware they run on. I designed Car Sounds, a depthwise-separable CNN that diagnoses automotive faults from engine audio on a bare microcontroller; I built FAMAIL, a spatial-temporal data augmentation framework that reduces bias amplification in imitation-learning models; and I co-founded LARK Business Services, an AI-native platform for the merchant card processing industry.

I'm pursuing an M.S. in Big Data Analytics at San Diego State University (expected Spring 2027), after completing my B.S. in Computer Science there with a 4.0 major GPA, summa cum laude. I work as a research assistant in two SDSU labs — the Data-informed Construction Engineering (DiCE) Lab and a fairness-aware imitation-learning project. The throughline across all of it is the same: I like taking hard, ambiguous problems and turning them into systems that work.

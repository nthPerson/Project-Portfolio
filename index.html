About

I'm an applied AI engineer and researcher who builds systems where software meets the physical world — agentic AI pipelines, machine-learning models, and the robotics and edge hardware they run on. I co-authored BIM2RDT, an agentic AI safety-first framework that turns building information models into robot-ready site digital twins, and I co-founded LARK Business Services, where I architected a multi-agent document-ingestion platform for the merchant card processing industry.

I'm pursuing an M.S. in Big Data Analytics at San Diego State University (expected Spring 2027), after completing my B.S. in Computer Science there with a 4.0 major GPA, summa cum laude. I work as a research assistant in two SDSU labs — the Data-informed Construction Engineering (DiCE) Lab and the FAMAIL fairness-aware imitation-learning project. The throughline across all of it is the same: I like taking hard, ambiguous problems and turning them into systems that work.
